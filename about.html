About Righting Wrongs

The band started off for a talent show at a local middle school, entering with the song All The Small Things by Blink-182. The original band name was Blink-129 due to short time to decide of a name.

After the talent show, the band decided to make it a permanent thing by changing the name to Righting Wrongs. In late 2010, Righting Wrongs was a strict cover band, performing covers by the Foo Fighters, All Time Low, Three Days Grace and Blink-182. The band continued on being a cover band until early 2012 when the idea of making a demo tape of original music came to mind.

On January 1, 2013, Righting Wrongs released their first full length EP featuring singles 'Static in a Radio' and 'Show Me You Care'.

In June 2012, Righting Wrongs released Demo 1, consisting of 4 songs; Static In A Radio, Life Without You, Not My Type, and Show Me You Care.
